POPULAR DESTINATIONS

Lisbon

The capital city of Portugal, Lisbon is known for its vibrant atmosphere, historic neighborhoods such as Alfama and Bairro Alto, stunning viewpoints, and iconic landmarks like São Jorge Castle, and Belém Tower. Visitors can also enjoy delicious Portuguese cuisine, lively nightlife, and cultural experiences.

Porto

Located in the north of Portugal, Porto is famous for its picturesque riverside setting, historic old town (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), and the production of port wine. Highlights include the Dom Luís I Bridge, Ribeira district, and Porto Cathedral. Wine tasting tours and boat cruises along the Douro are popular activities.

Read more
Braga

Known as the "Rome of Portugal," Braga is one of the country's oldest cities and a center of religious and cultural heritage. It boasts a wealth of historic monuments, including the Bom Jesus do Monte Sanctuary with its iconic Baroque stairway, Sé de Braga (Braga Cathedral), and the medieval Archbishop's Palace.

Read more
Sintra

Located just outside of Lisbon, Sintra is renowned for its fairytale-like palaces, lush gardens, and romantic atmosphere. Visitors can explore the colorful Pena Palace, the medieval Moorish Castle, and the extravagant Quinta da Regaleira estate with its mystical gardens and underground tunnels.

WHY PORTUGAL

Rich History and Culture

Portugal has a fascinating history that dates back thousands of years, with influences from various civilizations such as the Romans, Moors, and Celts. Visitors can explore well-preserved historic sites, medieval castles, charming old towns, and UNESCO World Heritage Sites, experiencing the country's cultural diversity firsthand.

Stunning Natural Beauty

From rugged coastlines to picturesque beaches, lush vineyards to scenic mountains, Portugal's diverse landscape offers breathtaking natural beauty. The country boasts stunning coastal cliffs, golden sand beaches, tranquil islands, verdant valleys, and charming countryside, providing ample opportunities for outdoor activities.

Delicious Cuisine and Wine

Portuguese cuisine is renowned for its fresh seafood, flavorful meats, hearty stews, and delectable pastries. Visitors can indulge in traditional dishes like bacalhau (salted cod), grilled sardines, pastéis de nata (custard tarts), and hearty soups. Portugal is also famous for its excellent wines, including port wine, and vinho verde.

Vibrant Cities and Charming Towns

Portugal's cities and towns offer a perfect blend of history, culture, and modern amenities. From the vibrant streets of Lisbon and Porto to the medieval charm of Sintra and Évora, each city and town has its own unique character and attractions, including historic landmarks, cultural institutions, bustling markets and lively nightlife.
